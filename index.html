Core Architecture Principles (v15.0)

In-Transit Visibility

We separate supply into confidence tiers: On Hand (100%), In-Transit (95%), and Ordered (70%). The `ASN` updates PO status to prevent planning churn.

The QC Firewall

Module 4 strictly separates Dock Receipt (Quantity) from Inspection (Quality). Only items passing `QC_Decision` enter the `Stock_Ledger`.

The Audit Pattern

Operational movements (M5) are decoupled from Financial Audits (M6). Daily receipts and issues happen in real-time, while `Stock_Reconciliation` posts periodic adjustments.

Transformation Pattern

Offline inputs follow a strict Draft → Staging → Validation → Final pipeline. Master Data is never edited directly.

Initial Setup (Admins)

Look for Purple Dashed rectangles Draft Doc. Download these system-generated lists (e.g., "Net Demand") to start your work.

2

Do Your Work (Offline)

Look for Yellow/Gold rounded rectangles Business Event. This is where you manipulate data offline (e.g., Excel, Python scripts).

3

Upload & Validate

Look for Green Hexagons Staging Data. This is the "Drop Box." Data enters here from two sources:

  • Internal: Your offline work (Yellow Events).
  • External: Real-world triggers like Invoices External.

Technical Specifications

  • [H] Tracks History (SCD2): Applied to Master Data and Final Documents. Records are versioned, never overwritten.
  • [A] Aggregated / Calculated: Views derived from logs (e.g., Stock_Data is sum of Stock_Ledger).
  • Project Scope: To-Be (Phase 2) Architecture - Inbound & Audit Decoupling.
