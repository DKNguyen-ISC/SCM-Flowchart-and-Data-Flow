MODULE 5: INVENTORY MOVEMENTS

Process Overview (v14.0)

This module executes the "day-to-day" inventory transactions. It is triggered by the **`QC_Decision`** from Module 4 and follows two distinct paths (Pass/Fail).
Key Logic: Every physical movement (Receipt, Quarantine, Transfer, Return) is recorded in the **`Stock_Ledger`**, which in turn updates the master **`Stock_Data`** (L1) for the M3 Balancing Hub to use.

1. Trigger: QC_Decision (from M4)

This module "wakes up" when M4's `QC_Decision` gateway sends it new data. We will use the same exception scenario from Module 4 for PO-100, Line 3 (`BOM_UPDATE: 300010406C`).
- **Input 1 (Pass):** 24,500 units triggered the `Material_Receipt` flow.
- **Input 2 (Fail):** 500 units triggered the `Stock_Quarantine` flow.

Process forks into two paths

Path A: The "Fail" Flow

Step 2a: `Stock_Quarantine` (Event)

The 500 failed units are logged and moved to a quarantine bin. Their status is `On Hand (Quarantined)`, making them unavailable for M3 planning.

Step 2b: `Return_To_Supplier` (Document)

This document is created to process the return. It sends a feedback update to `Purchase_Order` (M2) to record a "Rejected Qty" of 500. This is also used by M7 (Finance) to block payment for these units.

Path B: The "Pass" Flow

Step 3a: `Material_Receipt` (Event)

The 24,500 passed units are officially received. This event sends the *final* "Accepted Qty" feedback to `Purchase_Order` (M2), which M7 (Finance) needs for the 3-Way Match.

Step 3b: `Stock_Transfer` (Event)

This `Material_Receipt` immediately triggers an internal task to "put away" the items, moving them from the temporary QC bin to a final, permanent storage bin (`BIN_A-01`).

All movements are posted to the central ledger

4. Stock_Ledger (The "Bank Statement")

This is the **"Heart of M5"**. The `Stock_Ledger` is the immutable, chronological log of *all* inventory movements. It's like a bank statement for our warehouse. Notice the "debits" and "credits" that move our 25,000 units through the system.

...
The ledger updates the Master Data in real-time

5. Final Output: `Stock_Data` (Master) is Updated

This is the **final result of Module 5**. The `Stock_Ledger` (a log) constantly aggregates its data to update the `Stock_Data` (a master table) from Layer 1.
This table shows the *current state* of our inventory. The **M3 Balancing Hub** will read this "Available Qty" of 24,500 the next time it runs, completing the entire closed-loop P2P cycle.
