MODULE 4: INBOUND & QC

Process Overview (v13.0)

This module is the **bridge from digital to physical**. It manages the physical receipt of goods from suppliers.
Key Logic: It separates the **Quantity Check** (at the dock) from the **Quality Check** (in the lab). This creates a "QC Firewall" to ensure only inspected, usable stock becomes available for planning in Module 3.

1. Advanced_Shipping_Notification (ASN) [H]

This is the **trigger** for the inbound process. The supplier sends this document (e.g., packing slip) from M2, telling us what to expect on the truck. We are expecting all 4 lines from `PO-100`.

...
Triggers Physical Receipt (1:1)

2. Inbound_Shipment (Quantity Check)

The truck arrives. The warehouse team counts the physical items and reconciles them against the ASN. This is the **Quantity Check**.
Exception Scenario: For Line 3, the ASN said 26,000 units, but we only counted **25,000**. This "Short-Shipment" is recorded, and the `Purchase_Order` status is updated immediately.

...
Triggers (1:1)

3. Material_Inspection (Quality Check)

The items that passed the quantity check (e.g., the 25,000 units from Line 3) are moved to the QC area for a **Quality Check**.
Exception Scenario: The QC team inspects the 25,000 units and finds that **500** are damaged and fail inspection. This creates a "Pass/Fail" result.

...
Results in (1:1)

4. QC_Decision (Gateway)

This is the **"Control Gate"** and the end of M4. It is not a table, but a logical "fork in the road" that directs the inventory to Module 5 based on the inspection results. This creates the "QC Firewall."

PASS Qty > 0 (24,500 units):
This quantity is now approved. It triggers the `Material_Receipt` event in M5, moving the stock to "Available" status. This is the *only* stock the M3 Balancing Hub can use for planning.

FAIL Qty > 0 (500 units):
This quantity is rejected. It triggers the `Stock_Quarantine` event in M5. This stock is unavailable and will be returned to the supplier.

End of M4. Output feeds M5 Inventory
